How this site is built

Radical Collaboration Transparency

This page explains the philosophy behind cameronloudon.com, how it was built, and why I've chosen to work openly with AI rather than hide it or over-explain it.

The Origin Story

On 17 May 2026, I opened a Claude.ai session to ask a simple question: "Where can I host my personal website?"

What followed was a single, uninterrupted conversation that produced a personal brand philosophy, a content architecture, a named framework for AI collaboration transparency, a founding document, and this site.

No prior plan. No brief. One question, one conversation, one brain — and something I hadn't anticipated emerged.

I've worked with AI across many platforms and sessions over the years. It helps me get ideas out in real time — ideas that would otherwise stay trapped in my head or scattered across notes, chats and documents I'll never find again. I have a context window limitation too. This site is the solution to that problem.

The framework we named that day is called Radical Collaboration Transparency. This page is its first public expression.

What it is

Radical Collaboration Transparency is a personal publishing framework built on one principle:

AI collaboration is an intellectual influence like any other — and should be attributed honestly, not hidden or over-explained.

Most people either hide AI involvement or over-disclaim it. RCT does neither. It treats AI the same way a thoughtful person treats any significant intellectual influence — a mentor, a book, a conversation that changed your thinking. You name it. You credit it. You show your work.

What it means in practice

Every piece of content on this site that emerged from or was shaped by an AI session carries a Collaboration Note — a footer that states the model, session date, approximate tokens used, duration, and that the output was reviewed and approved by me. I value telemetry. I default to more detail rather than less.

This is not a disclaimer. It is an attribution.

What it is not

It is not about humanising AI output to disguise it. It is not performative disclosure for compliance. It is not a claim that AI wrote everything. It is a genuine practice of showing how I think — and who, and what, I think with.

The Master Synthesis Prompt

This site is built session by session. Each session starts by pasting a single document — the Master Synthesis Prompt — into a fresh AI conversation. It carries the full context of who I am, what I'm building, what has been decided, and how I want to work. It's my portable creative memory across AI instances, platforms, and time.

It is published here in full because transparency without evidence isn't transparency.
