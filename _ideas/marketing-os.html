Strategic Marketing Operating System

A reusable strategic operating system for university marketing functions — structured as a library of skill files that anchor all execution to rigorous strategic foundations.

Status Foundation Layer Complete
First built May 2026
Sector Higher Education
Collaborator Claude (Anthropic)

The Problem This Solves

Most university marketing functions operate without a coherent strategic operating system. Campaigns are launched, channels are managed, content is produced — but rarely anchored to a shared strategic foundation that every team member and every AI tool can reference.

The result is inconsistency. Generic communications. Brand erosion. Execution that moves fast but in the wrong direction.

This framework solves that by establishing the strategic foundation first — before any execution begins.

A key structural insight from the build: measurement is not a section — it is a thread running through every skill, with all measurement rolling up to the foundation objectives. This reframing emerged through dialogue, not declared at the outset.

The Foundation Layer

The six Foundation skills define the strategic operating system. They are not a document to be filed — they are a living reference that every execution decision checks against.

Each skill follows a consistent structure: purpose, substantive strategic sections, [TO BE DEFINED] fields marking the research agenda, and an AI Collaborator Instructions block that specifies what to check before producing output and when to flag and not proceed.

The [TO BE DEFINED] fields are not gaps. They are the research agenda — questions that must be answered before the execution layer can operate with confidence.

Who This Is For

Any university marketing function that wants to operate with strategic rigour — whether building a marketing capability from scratch, rebuilding after a leadership change, or establishing the strategic rails needed to work effectively with AI execution tools.

The framework is sector-specific to higher education but the architecture — Foundation → Execution → Measurement, with AI Collaborator Instructions embedded throughout — is transferable to any complex marketing function.
